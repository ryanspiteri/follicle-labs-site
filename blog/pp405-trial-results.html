PP405 Phase 2a Trial Results — What the Science Actually Shows

Follicle Labs Research Library · April 2026

The PP405 Phase 2a trial is the most significant piece of evidence for MPC inhibition as a hair follicle reactivation mechanism in humans. The results, registered under NCT06393452 on ClinicalTrials.gov, have been cited widely in the research community and represent the strongest clinical signal this class of compounds has produced.

This post examines the trial in detail: what was studied, how it was designed, what the results show, what they do not show, and what Phase 3 development means for the timeline.

What Is PP405?

PP405 is a small molecule compound developed by Pelage Pharmaceuticals, a biotechnology company commercialising research originating from the laboratory of Dr. William Lowry at UCLA. The compound inhibits the mitochondrial pyruvate carrier (MPC), a protein complex that regulates how cells metabolise pyruvate.

In hair follicle stem cells (HFSCs), MPC activity governs the metabolic state that keeps stem cells quiescent. By inhibiting MPC, PP405 shifts the energy metabolism of HFSCs toward glycolysis, a state associated with stem cell activation and proliferation. Activated HFSCs initiate the hair growth cycle.

This is mechanistically distinct from all existing approved treatments. Minoxidil acts through vasodilation. Finasteride acts through DHT reduction. PP405 acts on the intrinsic metabolic programme of the stem cells inside the follicle.

Trial Design: NCT06393452

The Phase 2a trial was registered as NCT06393452 on ClinicalTrials.gov. Key design parameters:

Participants: 78 adults with androgenetic alopecia (pattern hair loss affecting both male and female participants). Adults with a range of alopecia severity were included, providing data across the progression spectrum.

Treatment period: 4 weeks of topical PP405 application. The compound was applied as a topical formulation to affected scalp areas on a defined schedule.

Follow-up period: 12 weeks post-treatment. This extended observation window was critical for understanding whether any effects were transient or represented durable changes in follicle biology.

Primary outcome measures: Hair density assessment, including phototrichogram analysis and clinical photography. Ki67 staining (cell proliferation marker) was used in some analyses to confirm cellular-level HFSC activation.

Comparator: The trial included a vehicle control (carrier without active compound) to isolate the effect of PP405 specifically.

This was a controlled human study with objective measurement endpoints. Not a case series. Not anecdotal reports.

What the Results Actually Showed

The Headline: 31% Responders at Week 8

The most-cited result from the trial: 31% of participants demonstrated greater than 20% increase in hair density at the week 8 assessment.

Several points matter here.

First, the 8-week timepoint is only four weeks after treatment ended. The four-week treatment window was followed by a 12-week observation period, and the week 8 measurement falls within that follow-up phase. Hair density was still improving after the compound was no longer being applied. This is consistent with a biological mechanism that triggers follicle cycling rather than simply maintaining it while the compound is present.

Second, 20% density increase is a clinically meaningful threshold. In hair restoration research, a 10% increase in hair count is generally considered a relevant response. 20% exceeds that threshold substantially. The 31% responder rate at this threshold means approximately one in three participants achieved a significant outcome.

Third, the result is a responder analysis. The full distribution of outcomes is broader. Some participants exceeded 20% density increase substantially. The 31% figure represents those above the defined threshold, not the average effect across all participants.

Terminal Hair Growth in Previously Bald Areas

Among the more striking findings: some participants demonstrated terminal hair growth in areas that had been clinically bald. Terminal hairs are thick, pigmented, fully cycling hairs. Their presence in bald areas indicates follicle reactivation, not just improved density in thinning zones.

This is a qualitatively different finding from minoxidil outcomes. Minoxidil does not produce terminal hair growth in bald areas. It extends the growth phase of follicles that are still partially active. Terminal hair in a bald area means a dormant follicle re-entered the growth cycle and produced a hair that reached terminal diameter.

This outcome was not universal. It was observed in a subset of participants. But its presence in the data set is significant because it demonstrates that the MPC inhibition mechanism can reach fully quiescent follicles, not just those in early miniaturisation.

The 12-Week Follow-Up: Durability of Effect

The trial included measurements across the full follow-up period, not just the week 8 snapshot. The data showed that density improvements persisted and, in many cases, continued to develop throughout the 12-week follow-up.

This durability is mechanistically expected. When HFSCs are activated and enter the growth phase, the hair cycle proceeds on its own timeline, which is weeks to months. A brief period of MPC inhibition can trigger a cycling event that then runs to completion independently. This is different from minoxidil, which requires continuous application to maintain its vascular effects.

Whether a single treatment course produces lasting benefit or requires periodic reapplication is a key question for Phase 3.

Safety Profile

The Phase 2a trial did not report serious adverse events attributable to PP405. Local tolerability was acceptable, with minor scalp sensations in a small proportion of participants. No systemic effects consistent with hormonal interference (as seen with finasteride) were observed, which is mechanistically expected given that PP405 does not interact with the hormonal pathway.

The safety data from Phase 2a supported progression to Phase 3.

What the Trial Does Not Show

It is important to be precise about the scope of this evidence.

The Phase 2a trial was not a large-scale, double-blind, Phase 3 study with regulatory submission data. It is an efficacy signal study. The sample size (78 participants) is consistent with Phase 2a objectives: establishing that the mechanism works in humans, establishing an initial dose-response relationship, and identifying the responder population.

Phase 2a does not establish long-term safety at scale. It does not establish optimal dosing across the full severity spectrum of androgenetic alopecia. It does not compare PP405 directly against approved treatments in a head-to-head design.

The results are genuinely promising. They are not, by themselves, proof of equivalent or superior clinical outcome versus established treatments. That evidence requires Phase 3.

Anyone citing these results should cite them accurately, including the responder rate (31%), the density threshold (20%), and the assessment timepoint (week 8), without overstating what Phase 2a data can establish.

Phase 3: What It Means and the Expected Timeline

Phase 3 trials are the final pre-submission clinical stage. They require:

  • Larger participant numbers (typically hundreds to thousands)
  • Randomised, double-blind, controlled design
  • Multiple clinical sites
  • Primary endpoints agreed with regulators (FDA, EMA, TGA)
  • Long-term safety monitoring

For PP405, Phase 3 would need to establish:

  • Superiority or non-inferiority versus an active comparator or placebo at a defined endpoint
  • Safety in a large population over a clinically relevant duration
  • Dose optimisation data

Based on standard drug development timelines, Phase 3 initiation for PP405 is projected within two to three years of Phase 2a completion. Full Phase 3 data, if the trial runs on schedule, would be available four to six years from initiation. Regulatory review and approval would follow from there.

This means PP405 as an approved prescription treatment is likely a decade-scale proposition from now, not an immediate availability.
