JXL-069 is a research-grade mitochondrial pyruvate carrier (MPC) inhibitor formulated for topical application in hair follicle stem cell research. It is produced at 0.05% concentration in a 30mL spray delivery system.

What This Is

JXL-069 targets the same mechanistic pathway as PP405 — the compound developed by Pelage Pharmaceuticals and validated in a Phase 2a clinical trial (NCT06393452) registering 31% of participants with greater than 20% hair density increase at week 8.

The mitochondrial pyruvate carrier (MPC) is a protein complex on the inner mitochondrial membrane. It regulates pyruvate import into the mitochondria — a key step in cellular energy metabolism.

In Hair Follicle Stem Cells

In hair follicle stem cells (HFSCs), high MPC activity maintains a metabolic state associated with quiescence. Quiescent HFSCs do not divide. They do not initiate the hair growth cycle. Follicles with predominantly quiescent stem cells do not produce visible hair.

MPC Inhibition

Inhibiting MPC shifts the metabolic state of HFSCs toward glycolysis. This metabolic shift is associated with stem cell activation and proliferation. Activated HFSCs initiate the hair growth cycle, driving follicles from telogen (resting) toward anagen (growth).

This is a fundamentally different mechanism from minoxidil (vasodilation), finasteride (DHT reduction), or PRP (growth factor delivery). It operates at the cellular metabolic level, upstream of the factors those treatments address.

Application

  • Part hair to expose target scalp area
  • Apply 4–6 sprays to the target zone
  • Gently distribute with fingertips. Do not rinse.
  • Apply twice daily — morning and evening
  • Allow 4 hours between application and any other topical products

Research Cycle

  • Active treatment phase: 4 weeks
  • Observation period: 12 weeks post-treatment
  • Document baseline and weekly progress with consistent photography

Stacking

JXL-069 is mechanistically distinct from minoxidil. Some researchers apply both — JXL-069 in the morning (MPC inhibition) and minoxidil in the evening (vasodilation/anagen extension). Allow at least 4 hours between applications.
