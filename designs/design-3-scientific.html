// Mechanism of Action

Step-by-step pathway

01

Mitochondrial Pyruvate Carrier inhibition

MPC-1 selectively binds to and inhibits the mitochondrial pyruvate carrier, the protein complex responsible for transporting pyruvate across the inner mitochondrial membrane in follicle stem cells.

02

Metabolic state shift

MPC inhibition forces a shift in cellular metabolism away from oxidative phosphorylation toward glycolysis, a state associated with stem cell activation and proliferative activity.

03

Follicle stem cell exit from quiescence

The metabolic shift is sufficient to push quiescent hair follicle stem cells toward division and differentiation, disrupting the dormancy state characteristic of telogen phase.

04

Anagen phase re-entry and density increase

Activated follicle stem cells re-enter the anagen (active growth) phase, producing measurable increases in hair density over the study period.
