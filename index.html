Mechanism

How It Works

Minoxidil extends the growth phase. Finasteride slows DHT. Neither can reactivate a dormant follicle. MPC inhibition targets the metabolic switch inside the stem cell itself.

1

MPC Inhibition

JXL-069 inhibits the mitochondrial pyruvate carrier (MPC) — the metabolic gatekeeper that keeps hair follicle stem cells (HFSCs) in a dormant, quiescent state.

2

Metabolic Switch

Inhibiting the MPC shifts cellular energy metabolism away from oxidative phosphorylation toward glycolysis — the same metabolic state seen in actively cycling follicles.

3

Terminal Hair Growth

The metabolic shift triggers HFSC activation. Activated stem cells re-enter the hair growth cycle. Dormant follicles begin producing terminal hair again.
